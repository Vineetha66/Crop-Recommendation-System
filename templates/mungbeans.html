MUNG BEANS

Mung beans, also known as green gram or moong dal, are a type of legume belonging to the family Fabaceae.

They are small, green, oval-shaped beans commonly used in Asian cuisine, particularly in Indian, Chinese, and Southeast Asian dishes.

Mung beans are widely cultivated in various parts of Asia, including India, China, and Southeast Asian countries.

They thrive in warm climates with well-drained soil and are often grown as a rotation crop to improve soil fertility.

Mung beans are highly nutritious and are a rich source of protein, dietary fiber, vitamins, and minerals.

They are used in a variety of dishes such as soups, stews, salads, curries, and desserts.
